# Perplexity Discovery Prompts: Building Site Authority

Use these specifically engineered prompts in [Perplexity.ai](https://www.perplexity.ai) to find high-authority websites where you can list your new site or request backlinks.

## 1. The "Competitor Backlink" Hunter
**Goal**: Find websites that already link to your competitors. If they link to one LGBTQ+ real estate site, they are likely to link to yours.

> **Prompt**: 
> "Identify the top 10 websites, blogs, or directories that link to or mention 'gayrealestate.com' or 'naglrep.com' specifically in the context of Connecticut real estate. For each site found, tell me:
> 1. The domain name and its relevance to LGBTQ+ housing.
> 2. Whether it's a directory, a news site, or a blog.
> 3. Search for a 'Contact Us' or 'Submit a Link' page for each one."

## 2. The "Local CT LGBTQ+ Authority" Agent
**Goal**: Find high-authority local organizations in Connecticut that have "trust" in the eyes of Google.

> **Prompt**: 
> "Find the most active and reputable LGBTQ+ community organizations, pride event organizers, and local news outlets (like CT Insider or Hartford Courant) that have sections or articles dedicated to 'LGBTQ+ living in Connecticut'. List the specific URLs for their 'Resource' pages or 'Community Partners' sections where a professional real estate network might be listed."

## 3. The "AEO / Bot Proof" Validation
**Goal**: See how bots currently summarize the CT LGBTQ+ real estate market and identify "gaps" you can fill.

> **Prompt**: 
> "Act as a researcher for a gay couple moving from NYC to West Hartford, CT. Search the web and provide a list of the 5 most reliable sources for finding LGBTQ+ friendly real estate agents and attorneys in CT. Specifically check if 'GayRealEstateCT.net' appears in any recent search results or AI summaries. If it does not appear, explain what sources the current results are prioritizing."

## 4. The "Mortgage & Legal" Connection
**Goal**: Since you have Jake Earl and Carolyn Futtner on the team, find places where their specific expertise is mentioned.

> **Prompt**: 
> "Search for 'inclusive mortgage lenders in Connecticut' and 'LGBTQ+ real estate attorneys CT'. List the top 10 articles or directories that appear. For each, check if there is a way to submit a new professional profile or if they accept guest contributions on property law/financing for the queer community."

---

### How to use these results:
1.  **Check for "Add Listing" buttons**: Many directories have a free or paid "Add Listing" option.
2.  **Reach out to Editors**: For news sites or blogs, use the contact info found to send a short pitch: *"I saw your resource guide for LGBTQ+ CT residents. We've launched a new vetted hub for agents, lenders, and attorneys that might be a great addition for your readers."*
3.  **Forward to the Team**: Have Arek or Jake reach out to organizations they are already members of (like the CT LGBTQ+ Real Estate Alliance) and ensure the link is active there.
